About

I am a PhD student in mathematical biology at the University of Freiburg, interested in stochastic processes, population genetics, and epidemiology. My research focuses on understanding complex biological systems through quantitative modeling, particularly in stochastic models, fast-slow processes, Markov processes, and martingale problems.

My current research includes modelling TE (transposable element) abundance and their dynamics in biological systems. I am passionate about applying rigorous mathematical frameworks to solve real-world biological problems.

  • Stochastic processes and mathematical biology
  • Population genetics and evolutionary dynamics
  • Epidemiological modeling
  • Markov chains and martingale theory
Research Interests
  • Stochastic Models: Applying stochastic processes to understand biological variability
  • Population Genetics: Modeling genetic variation and evolution in populations
  • Epidemiology: Disease dynamics and transmission modeling
